As a geospatial data analyst, I have 15 years of experience leveraging remote sensing data and geographic information systems to study environmental change. I enjoy telling stories about history through imagery and cartography. I currently teach cartography and Geographic Object-Based Image Analysis at Michigan State University. In addition to this academic work, I am an avid landscape photographer and visual artist. I enjoy working in watercolor, ceramic, and stained-glass mediums to express the essence of the physical environment.
